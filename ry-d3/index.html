"Unignorable Threat: The Rising Prevalence of Burglary in Victoria"

Introduction

Australia, often proud as one of the safest countries in the Western world, has an incongruously high incidence of burglaries and break-ins. Surprisingly, based on private and governmental security statistics, Australia ranks seventh globally for the number of burglaries, and this rank has kept increasing. Victoria, the country's most populous state, is not exempt from this challenge. Recent data from the Victoria Crime Statistics Bureau indicates a concerning uptick in burglary-related incidents over the past year, with a notable surge of over 80% in burglaries involving juveniles (See more details). This report delves into this problematic trend, aiming to dissect its underlying causes and help residents assess the safety of their homes.

Crime Map

A common misconception is equating a local government area's (LGA) overall crime rate with its burglary rate. An LGA with a low crime rate might still experience a high incidence of burglaries. Regional economic conditions, community surveillance levels, and available police resources play significant roles. Simply relying on crime rate maps often doesn't provide insightful data on burglary-specific trends, necessitating a deeper focus on the unique characteristics and changes of burglaries.

Select LGAs
In Victoria, burglary rates vary significantly across LGAs, highlighting the crucial role of community vigilance in curbing such incidents. Intruders typically shy away from areas with dense populations due to the heightened risk of detection. For those residing in LGAs with elevated burglary frequencies, particularly in locales characterized by abundant foliage and sparse neighbours, extra precautions are necessary. Beyond property damage, burglaries can escalate into violent crimes, jeopardizing personal safety. Conclusion and suggestions

What is certain is that crime rates in Victoria, especially the number of burglaries, have rebounded significantly. Even if there are no valuables like jewellery in the house, it can attract intruders. As residents of Victoria, it is important for us to be prepared and safe for the possibility of more burglaries in the future, especially those in high-crime LGAs. Here are some actionable suggestions:
Strengthen physical protection
Install protective doors, windows and locks, and ensure all entrances have appropriate security equipment. Some people also choose to use iron bars or guardrails to enhance the security of their windows.
Vegetation Management
Ensure vegetation around your home is kept from providing a hiding place for burglars. Prune trees and shrubs regularly.

Network with neighbours
Starting a neighbourhood watch or maintaining good relationships with neighbours can help each other monitor homes and report suspicious activity.
Store and record valuables
Label your valuables and save their purchase receipts, photos or serial numbers. This action will make it easier to retrieve the item if it has been stolen.
Following the advice above can significantly reduce the risk of burglarising your home.
